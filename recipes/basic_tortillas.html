Flour Tortillas

Ingredients

  • 2 & 1/2 cups All Purpose Flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 cup of water warmed to 115F
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/3 cup Shortening or Lard

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, place the flour, baking powder, and salt. Using a fork, pastry blender, or your hands, incorporate the shortening or lard until the mixture resembles a coarse meal.
  2. Slowly add the hot water a little at a time until the dough holds together. Only add a small amount of water at once.
  3. Transfer the dough onto a work surface and knead for a couple of minutes until smooth.
  4. Divide the dough into 10 equal portions, rolling each piece on a lightly floured surface with the palm of your hand to form a small disk.
  5. Cover the dough balls (also called testales de harina) with a damp clean kitchen towel, plastic wrap, or parchment paper.
  6. Let the dough rest for 30–45 minutes to develop gluten and make rolling easier.
  7. After resting, preheat an ungreased griddle, cast-iron skillet, or comal over medium heat.
  8. Lightly flour your surface and begin forming tortillas with a rolling pin. Avoid adding too much flour or the tortillas will be dry.
  9. Place a dough ball on the surface, press slightly, and roll:
    1. Roll forward from the center without reaching the edge.
    2. Roll backward toward yourself without reaching the edge.
    3. Rotate the dough 45° and repeat. Flip and continue until a thin ~10" disk forms.
  10. Place the tortilla on the hot griddle — cook 20–30 seconds until air bubbles form and light golden spots appear.
  11. Flip and cook another ~20 seconds as more bubbles form.
  12. Flip a final time; the tortilla should puff up in ~10 seconds.
  13. Remove the tortilla and wrap it in a kitchen towel. Continue cooking the remaining tortillas and stack them wrapped to keep warm.
